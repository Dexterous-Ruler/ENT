# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indian MSME owners: kirana store operators, clinic managers, coaching centre administrators, mechanics, and travel agents. Non-technical, smartphone-native, operating in regional languages (Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Bengali, Kannada, Gujarati, Odia). They manage customer interactions through WhatsApp, not email or web apps. Most are on budget Android devices with 4G connectivity. Their primary job: reduce missed customer queries, automate appointment booking and FAQs, and appear professional without hiring additional staff.

Secondary user: the end customer calling or messaging the MSME's WhatsApp number in their mother tongue, expecting natural, helpful responses.

## Product Purpose

Sarvam DeployBridge is a no-code deployment platform that lets any Indian MSME owner configure, deploy, and manage an AI-powered WhatsApp business assistant in their regional language, in under 15 minutes, with zero technical knowledge. It bridges the gap between Sarvam AI's powerful Indic AI infrastructure and the 63 million MSMEs who cannot access it today. Success looks like: an MSME owner going from zero to a working, customer-facing AI assistant in one sitting, then returning daily to the dashboard to see conversation summaries, leads, and business insights in their own language.

## Brand Personality

Practical, empowering, trustworthy. This is not a Silicon Valley product talking down to small business owners. It is a tool that respects their intelligence, speaks their language (literally), and gets out of their way. The tone is confident but warm, like a knowledgeable local business advisor. No jargon, no condescension.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with blue gradients and abstract illustrations (Intercom, HubSpot onboarding). These feel foreign to the target user.
- Overly playful or cartoon-heavy UIs (Duolingo-style). The user is running a business, not playing a game.
- Dark-mode-first developer tools (Linear, Raycast). The users are not developers and use their phones outdoors in bright light.
- Enterprise-heavy interfaces with 50 menu items (Salesforce). Cognitive overload kills adoption.
- Crypto/fintech dark neon aesthetics. Completely wrong register.

## Design Principles

1. **Speak their language, literally.** Every label, every error message, every empty state must work in Hindi and English. The UI must never feel like a translation of an English product.
2. **15 minutes or it failed.** The onboarding flow is the product's signature. If setup takes longer than a chai break, we have failed. Every screen must push the user toward completion, never pause them.
3. **Glanceable over explorable.** The dashboard serves MSME owners checking their phone between customers. Key metrics must be readable in 3 seconds. Drill-down is secondary.
4. **Earn trust through transparency.** Show the user exactly what their customers will experience. The WhatsApp preview during onboarding is not a feature; it is a trust mechanism.
5. **Built for sunlight.** Most users will view this on a phone screen in bright ambient light. High contrast, generous touch targets, no hover-dependent interactions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum, with strong contrast ratios suitable for outdoor mobile use
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px (mobile-first users)
- No hover-only interactions (touch-first audience)
- Support for Devanagari and other Indic scripts with proper font rendering
- Reduced motion support for all animations
- Screen reader support for all interactive elements
